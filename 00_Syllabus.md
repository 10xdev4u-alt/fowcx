# OE22708 FUNDAMENTALS OF WIRELESS COMMUNICATION
**L T P C: 3 0 0 3**

## COURSE OBJECTIVES:
- To introduce various generations of wireless systems.
- To acquaint fundamentals of cellular systems design.
- To familiarize with various multiple access schemes used in wireless communication.
- To introduce mobile radio propagation.
- To provide basic knowledge of diversity and equalization techniques.

---

## UNIT I: WIRELESS COMMUNICATION SYSTEMS (9 periods)
Generation of wireless communication systems: Examples of wireless systems: Cordless, Paging Systems. Cellular Telephone System, Comparison of wireless systems, Personal Communication Systems, Call establishment in cellular systems.

## UNIT II: FUNDAMENTALS OF CELLULAR COMMUNICATION (9 periods)
Frequency reuse, Handoff, Channel Assignment, Interference and system capacity, improving coverage and capacity in cellular systems: cell splitting, sectoring, repeaters for range extensions, microcell zone concept.

## UNIT III: MULTIPLE ACCESS TECHNIQUES (9 periods)
Introduction to Multiple Access Techniques - FDMA, TDMA, Spread Spectrum Multiple Access - FHMA, CDMA, Basics of OFDM.

## UNIT IV: MOBILE RADIO PROPAGATION (9 periods)
Introduction to Radio Wave Propagation - Large Scale Path Loss - Free Space Propagation Model - Propagation Mechanisms: Reflection, Diffraction, Scattering - Small Scale Multipath Propagation.

## UNIT V: DIVERSITY TECHNIQUES (9 periods)
Introduction to Diversity - Space Diversity - Selection Diversity - Feedback Diversity - Maximal Ratio Combining - Equal Gain Combining - Polarization Diversity - Frequency Diversity - Time Diversity.

**TOTAL: 45 PERIODS**
